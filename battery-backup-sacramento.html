Start with backup priorities

  • List the loads that matter most: refrigerator, internet, lights, garage door, medical devices, office equipment, or selected HVAC needs.
  • Decide whether the goal is essential-load backup or broader home backup.
  • Review battery placement, electrical panel setup, and load management.

How incentives fit

  • SMUDs program is designed around battery storage participation, not just standby backup.
  • SMUD states participants keep access to battery power during outages and that a reserve is maintained after events.
  • A visit can help balance incentive participation with backup priorities.

Common questions

Can a home battery run my whole house?

It depends on battery size, electrical design, load management, and what appliances are running during an outage.

Will SMUD use all of my battery?

SMUD says program participants have access to power during outages and that SMUD leaves a 20% reserve at the end of an event.

Is backup power the same as bill savings?

No. Backup goals and bill goals overlap, but they should be reviewed separately before equipment is selected.
