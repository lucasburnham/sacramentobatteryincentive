Have a recent SMUD bill and any existing solar documentation ready.

Questions existing solar owners should ask

  • Are you on the original Net Energy Metering rate or already on the Solar and Storage Rate?
  • Do you want a battery for backup, incentive value, evening peak coverage, or all three?
  • Will the project require changes to the system size, inverter setup, or interconnection paperwork?

Why SMUD rate status matters

  • SMUD says many original NEM customers may remain on that rate through Dec. 31, 2030.
  • SMUD says current NEM customers who add battery storage and want My Energy Optimizer Partner+ incentives will be placed on SSR.
  • A property review helps compare incentive value against rate and system-design changes.

Common questions

Can I add a battery if I already have solar?

Often yes, but the right path depends on existing equipment, SMUD rate status, interconnection, and program eligibility.

Will adding a battery affect NEM status?

SMUD says existing NEM customers who add battery storage to receive incentives will be placed on the Solar and Storage Rate.

Should I book before picking a battery?

Yes. A review before equipment selection can help avoid missing program, rate, or backup-design details.
